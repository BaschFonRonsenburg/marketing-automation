Limitations & How to Beat the Market

Where Meridian is losing ground

  • No online subscription. Recurring DTC revenue is left on the table — all three rivals sell beyond the counter.
  • No content engine. Zero brew guides or origin stories, so you're invisible for “single origin Asheville” searches Fathom already ranks for.
  • Punch-card-only loyalty. No email capture means no list to grow — competitors run data-backed rewards.
  • Thin social cadence. Great product, inconsistent storytelling versus Fathom's daily origin posts.
  • No wholesale page. Local cafés and offices can't find or contact you; two rivals capture that B2B demand.

Prioritized moves

  1. Launch a single-origin subscription. Put a “meet the farm” card in every box — turn your transparency edge into recurring revenue.
  2. Build a brew-guide + origin content hub. Target the exact keywords Fathom and Copper Kettle already rank for.
  3. Move loyalty to email-backed rewards. Capture addresses at the café to build the list your subscription launch needs.
  4. Add a wholesale landing page. A simple inquiry form reclaims local café and office accounts.
The angle the campaign runs on
